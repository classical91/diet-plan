Herbs & Spices — How to Use
Methods of preparation, optimal dosing, and best uses for culinary and medicinal herbs and spices.
Culinary Spices
Turmeric
Anti-inflammatory · Spice
Add to curries, soups, golden milk, and smoothies. Always combine with black pepper (piperine) to increase absorption by 2,000%.
  • Culinary: 1–3g / day (about 1/2 tsp)
  • Supplement: 500–1,000mg curcumin extract
  • Golden milk: 1 tsp turmeric + pinch black pepper + warm milk + honey
Heat stableFat-solublePairs with black pepper
Cayenne Pepper
Metabolism · Spice
Add to meals, sauces, or dissolve 1/8 tsp in warm lemon water on an empty stomach to stimulate circulation and metabolism.
  • Capsaicin activates TRPV1 receptors — boosts thermogenesis
  • Start with tiny amounts — 1/16 tsp — and build up
  • Topical cream: 0.025–0.075% capsaicin for joint pain
StimulantMetabolic
Cinnamon
Blood Sugar · Spice
Add 1/2–1 tsp to oatmeal, coffee, smoothies, or yogurt. Ceylon cinnamon is safer for daily use than Cassia (lower coumarin).
  • Take before carb-heavy meals to blunt blood sugar spike
  • Use Ceylon variety for daily supplementation
  • Dose: 1–6g / day for blood sugar effects
Ceylon preferredPre-meal
Ginger
Digestion · Nausea · Anti-inflammatory
Use fresh, powdered, or as tea. Grate fresh ginger into meals, teas, or juices. Dried ginger is 5–10x more concentrated in shogaols.
  • Nausea: 1g fresh ginger or tea before travel
  • Anti-inflammatory: 2–3g/day fresh or 1g dried
  • Digestive: chew a small piece before meals
  • Pairs with lemon and honey in tea
Fresh or driedHeat stable
Garlic
Antimicrobial · Cardiovascular
Crush and let sit 10 minutes before cooking — this activates allicin. Eat raw for maximum benefit. Cooking reduces but doesn't eliminate benefits.
  • Raw: 1–2 cloves / day for immune and cardiovascular effects
  • Aged garlic extract: 600–1,200mg if sensitive to raw
  • Crush → rest 10 min → cook for preserved allicin
Crush firstRaw is strongest
Sage, Thyme & Oregano
Antimicrobial · Antioxidant
Use fresh or dried in cooking. Oil of oregano is a concentrated supplement — very different from culinary oregano.
  • Sage tea: 2g dried sage in hot water — supports memory
  • Thyme tea: soothes cough and respiratory infections
  • Oil of oregano: 1–3 drops under tongue for antimicrobial use
  • Always dilute oil of oregano with carrier oil for topical use
Culinary dose safeOil = concentrated
Medicinal Herbs (How to Prepare)
Ashwagandha
Adaptogen · Cortisol · Stress
  • Powder: 1/2 tsp in warm milk (traditional Ayurvedic method — enhances absorption)
  • Capsule: 300–600mg KSM-66 or Sensoril extract (standardized)
  • Take at night — supports sleep quality
  • Cycle: 8 weeks on, 2 weeks off recommended
  • Takes 4–8 weeks to feel full effect
Holy Basil (Tulsi)
Adaptogen · Anxiety · Blood Sugar
  • Tea: steep 5–7 fresh leaves or 1 tsp dried in hot water for 5 min
  • Capsule: 300–600mg standardized extract
  • Drink 1–3 cups daily for stress and blood sugar support
  • Safe for daily long-term use — gentler than ashwagandha
Milk Thistle
Liver Protection · Detox
  • Capsule: 140–200mg silymarin (standardized to 70–80%)
  • Tea: 1 tsp crushed seeds steeped 10 min — less potent than extract
  • Take 20 min before meals for best liver protection
  • Safe for long-term use — often taken for months during detox
Dandelion
Liver · Kidney · Digestion
  • Root tea: roast and steep for liver support (2 tsp dried root / 8oz water)
  • Leaf tea: diuretic, rich in vitamins (1 tbsp dried leaves)
  • Tincture: 2–8ml three times daily
  • Whole plant is usable — root, leaf, and flower
Echinacea
Immune Boost · Antiviral
  • Tea: 1–2 tsp dried herb steeped 15 min — drink 3x/day during illness
  • Tincture: 2–3ml 3x/day at onset of cold
  • Capsule: 300–500mg dried extract 3x/day
  • Best taken at first sign of illness — not for continuous prevention
  • Cycle: use for max 10 days, break 3 days, repeat if needed
Ginseng
Energy · Cognitive · Adaptogen
  • Tea: 2–3g dried root simmered 15 min in 2 cups water
  • Capsule: 200–400mg standardized extract (morning or early afternoon)
  • Tincture: 2–4ml 2x/day — Asian and American differ in effect
  • Asian ginseng: energizing. American ginseng: calming. Siberian: adaptogen
  • Avoid taking late in day — can affect sleep
Tinctures — What They Are & How to Make Them
A tincture is a concentrated herbal extract made by soaking herbs in alcohol or glycerin. Far more potent than teas and lasts for years.
How to Make a Basic Tincture
Method: Folk method (ratio method) — ideal for home preparation. No precise measuring required.
1
Choose your herbFresh herbs: fill jar loosely. Dried herbs: fill jar 1/2 full. Roots/bark: coarsely chop and fill 1/3–1/2 jar.
2
Choose your menstruum (solvent)Alcohol (vodka or grain alcohol 40–60% ABV): best for most herbs. Glycerin: alcohol-free option — less potent, sweeter. Apple cider vinegar: mild — for culinary herbs.
3
CombineFill jar with herbs. Pour menstruum until herbs are fully submerged with 1–2 inches of liquid above. Seal tightly with lid.
4
MacerateStore in a cool dark place for 4–6 weeks. Shake daily if possible. Fresh herbs: 4 weeks. Dried herbs and roots: 6–8 weeks.
5
Strain and bottleStrain through cheesecloth or fine mesh. Press all liquid out of the plant material. Bottle in dark glass dropper bottles. Label with herb, date, and menstruum.
6
Store and useAlcohol tinctures: 5–10 year shelf life. Glycerin: 1–3 years. Typical dose: 1–4ml (20–80 drops) in water, 1–3x daily. Always start with lowest dose.
Popular Tinctures & Their Uses
Valerian Root
Sleep · Anxiety
Strong sedative tincture. Strongly flavored — mix in juice or strong tea to mask taste.
  • Dose: 2–4ml 30–60 min before bed
  • Works best after 2–4 weeks of consistent use
  • Do not combine with alcohol or sedative medications
Elderberry
Immune · Antiviral
Elderberry tincture or syrup — one of the most effective natural antivirals for respiratory illness.
  • Dose: 1–2ml 3–4x daily during illness
  • Prevention: 1ml daily during flu season
  • Syrup: 1 tbsp daily (easier for children)
Licorice Root
Gut · Adrenal · Antiviral
Soothing to gut lining. Supports adrenal function. Use DGL (deglycyrrhizinated) form if concerned about blood pressure.
  • Dose: 1–3ml 3x daily before meals for gut issues
  • Short-term use: max 4–6 weeks without break
  • Avoid in high blood pressure unless using DGL form
Frankincense (Boswellia)
Inflammation · Joint Pain
Available as resin, capsule, or tincture. Powerful for arthritis and inflammatory conditions.
  • Tincture: 2–4ml 2x daily for joint pain
  • Standardized extract: 300–400mg 2–3x daily
  • Apply diluted resin oil topically for localized joint pain
Rhodiola Rosea
Energy · Fatigue · Adaptogen
Best taken in the morning. Remarkable for mental and physical fatigue without stimulant side effects.
  • Tincture: 1–2ml in morning, fasted
  • Capsule: 200–600mg standardized (3% rosavins, 1% salidroside)
  • Cycle: 6–12 weeks on, 2 weeks off
Reishi Mushroom
Immune · Sleep · Anti-cancer
Dual-extract tincture (water + alcohol) captures both beta-glucans and triterpenes. Single extraction misses compounds.
  • Tincture: 2–3ml 2x daily
  • Best taken in evening — calming and sleep-supportive
  • Look for dual-extract on label
Aromatherapy — How to Use Essential Oils
Essential oils interact with the limbic system through the olfactory nerve — the only sense with direct brain access. Effects are fast (within 15 seconds of inhalation).
Methods of Use
Diffusion
Most Common Method
Add 5–15 drops to an ultrasonic diffuser with water. Run for 30–60 min, then break. Continuous diffusion reduces effectiveness.
  • Best for mood, sleep, and respiratory effects
  • Diffuse lavender at night for sleep
  • Diffuse peppermint or rosemary in morning for focus
  • Citrus oils for energy and mood elevation
Topical Application
Always Dilute First
Never apply essential oils undiluted to skin. Always dilute in a carrier oil (coconut, jojoba, almond) before skin contact.
  • Dilution ratio: 2% = 12 drops per 1 fl oz carrier oil
  • For children or sensitive skin: 0.5–1%
  • Apply to pulse points: wrists, temples, neck, behind ears
  • Test patch first — wait 24h for sensitivity reaction
Inhalation (Direct)
Fastest Effect
Add 1–2 drops to palms, cup over nose and breathe deeply for 3–5 breaths. Or use an inhaler stick.
  • Best for acute anxiety, nausea, headache
  • Peppermint: instant headache and nausea relief
  • Lavender: rapid anxiety reduction
  • Use inhaler sticks for on-the-go use
Bath & Steam
Full Body Absorption
Mix 5–10 drops with a tablespoon of carrier oil or bath salts before adding to bath — never add directly to water (oils float and can irritate skin).
  • Eucalyptus steam: 3–5 drops in bowl of hot water, cover head with towel
  • Bath: lavender + magnesium flakes for sleep and muscle recovery
  • Foot soak: 5 drops in warm water with Epsom salts

Neuro & Body Chemicals — Food & Lifestyle Links
Every mood, motivation, focus, and energy state is driven by specific chemicals. Here's how food and lifestyle directly influence each one.
Serotonin — Happiness & Calm
90% produced in gut · Mood · Sleep · Appetite
Serotonin is the foundation of emotional stability, contentment, and sleep quality.
  • Foods: tryptophan-rich foods — turkey, eggs, cheese, pumpkin seeds, salmon
  • Gut health: 90% of serotonin is made in the gut — probiotics are essential
  • Sunlight: natural serotonin synthesis — 20–30 min direct sun daily
  • Exercise: increases tryptophan crossing blood-brain barrier
  • Cofactors: B6, zinc, magnesium required for synthesis
  • Depleted by: chronic stress, alcohol, processed food, gut dysbiosis
Tryptophan → 5-HTP → Serotonin
Dopamine — Motivation & Reward
Drive · Pleasure · Focus · Addiction
Dopamine drives motivation, desire, and the reward cycle. Low dopamine = apathy, procrastination, and depression.
  • Foods: tyrosine-rich — chicken, eggs, almonds, avocado, bananas
  • Cold exposure: cold showers increase dopamine 250%
  • Exercise: intense cardio significantly boosts dopamine
  • Mucuna pruriens: natural L-DOPA precursor supplement
  • Cofactors: iron, B6, vitamin C required for synthesis
  • Depleted by: sugar, social media dopamine loops, sleep deprivation
Tyrosine → L-DOPA → Dopamine
Cortisol — Stress Hormone
Stress · Inflammation · Metabolism · Wakefulness
Cortisol peaks in the morning (normal) and should decline through the day. Chronically elevated cortisol devastates health.
  • Lower with: ashwagandha, rhodiola, holy basil, magnesium
  • Lower with: meditation (reduces cortisol 20%), cold exposure, sleep
  • Vitamin C in high doses reduces post-exercise cortisol spike
  • Phosphatidylserine: 400mg reduces exercise-induced cortisol
  • Elevated by: caffeine (especially before sleep), chronic stress, poor sleep, inflammatory diet
Adaptogens helpSleep is key
GABA — Calm & Relaxation
Anxiety relief · Inhibitory neurotransmitter · Sleep
GABA is the brain's primary inhibitory (calming) neurotransmitter. Low GABA = anxiety, racing thoughts, and insomnia.
  • Foods: fermented foods (increase GABA precursors), green tea (L-theanine)
  • L-theanine: 200–400mg promotes GABA and alpha brain waves without sedation
  • Magnesium: regulates GABA receptors — 400mg before bed
  • Valerian root: binds GABA receptors similarly to benzodiazepines
  • Depleted by: alcohol (short-term increases, long-term depletes), chronic stress
L-theanineMagnesium glycinate
Melatonin — Sleep Hormone
Circadian rhythm · Sleep onset · Antioxidant
Melatonin is produced from serotonin when darkness falls. Light exposure — especially blue light — suppresses production.
  • Foods: tart cherry juice, walnuts, eggs, fatty fish — natural melatonin sources
  • Block blue light 2 hours before bed (orange glasses)
  • Keep bedroom completely dark — even small light sources disrupt production
  • Supplement: 0.5–1mg is often more effective than 5–10mg doses
  • Depleted by: blue light, night shift work, alcohol, caffeine late in day
Oxytocin — Bonding & Trust
Connection · Love · Anti-anxiety · Wound healing
Oxytocin is released through touch, eye contact, social bonding, and orgasm. It counters cortisol and promotes healing.
  • Foods: magnesium and vitamin D support oxytocin signaling
  • Physical touch: hugging for 20+ seconds triggers oxytocin release
  • Eye contact, genuine conversation, petting animals all boost oxytocin
  • Exercise and cold water also stimulate release
  • Depleted by: social isolation, chronic stress, loneliness
Insulin — Blood Sugar Regulation
Glucose metabolism · Fat storage · Inflammation
Insulin sensitivity is one of the most important health markers. Insulin resistance drives obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and cognitive decline.
  • Improve with: cinnamon, berberine, apple cider vinegar before meals
  • Fasting: intermittent fasting dramatically improves insulin sensitivity
  • Exercise: muscle contraction absorbs glucose without insulin (walk after meals)
  • Chromium and magnesium improve insulin signaling
  • Impaired by: sugar, refined carbs, seed oils, sedentary lifestyle, poor sleep
Adrenaline (Epinephrine) — Fight or Flight
Emergency response · Focus under stress · Heart rate
Short-term adrenaline is normal and healthy. Chronically elevated adrenaline from stress, caffeine, or poor sleep damages health.
  • Regulate with: adaptogens (ashwagandha, rhodiola), breath work (4-7-8 breathing)
  • Cold exposure: brief cold triggers controlled adrenaline release for resilience
  • Magnesium: blunts excessive adrenaline response
  • Elevated by: caffeine, excessive exercise, chronic stress, processed sugar
Shakes — Recipes & Protocols
Protein shakes, detox shakes, recovery shakes, and mineral shakes with exact ingredients and preparation steps.
Protein Shakes
Classic Muscle Builder
High Protein · Post-Workout · ~40g protein
Ingredients
  • 1 scoop whey or plant protein (25–30g protein)
  • 1 cup whole milk or oat milk
  • 1 frozen banana
  • 1 tbsp almond butter
  • 1 tsp creatine monohydrate
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Ice to blend
Protocol
  • Consume within 30–60 min post-workout
  • Add leucine if using plant protein
  • Replace banana with berries for lower sugar
  • Add spinach (tasteless) for micronutrients
Keto Protein Shake
Low Carb · High Fat · ~35g protein
Ingredients
  • 1 scoop unflavored or vanilla whey
  • 1 cup full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp MCT oil
  • 1 tbsp almond butter
  • 1/2 avocado
  • Pinch of sea salt
  • Stevia to taste
Protocol
  • Keep carbs under 5g total
  • MCT oil promotes ketone production
  • Take in morning or post-workout
  • Add collagen peptides for joint support
Detox Shakes
Heavy Metal Detox Shake
Chlorophyll · Chelation · Morning Protocol
Ingredients
  • 1 cup wild blueberries (frozen)
  • 1 tsp spirulina powder
  • 1 tsp chlorella powder
  • 1 cup coconut water
  • 1 handful fresh cilantro
  • 1 tbsp Atlantic dulse (seaweed)
  • 1/2 cup water or ice
Protocol
  • Take on empty stomach in morning
  • Cilantro + chlorella chelate heavy metals
  • Spirulina binds and removes them from GI tract
  • Wild blueberries repair oxidative damage from metals
  • Cycle: 5 days on, 2 days off for 4–6 weeks
Liver Detox Green Shake
Liver Support · Anti-inflammatory · Daily Use
Ingredients
  • 2 cups spinach or kale
  • 1 cucumber
  • 1/2 lemon (juiced)
  • 1 inch fresh ginger
  • 1 inch fresh turmeric (or 1 tsp powder)
  • 1 cup water or coconut water
  • 1 green apple (optional sweetness)
Protocol
  • Drink on empty stomach 20 min before breakfast
  • Ginger and turmeric reduce liver inflammation
  • Lemon stimulates bile and liver enzyme production
  • Add milk thistle powder for enhanced liver protection

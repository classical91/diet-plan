Reference Guide

Types of Detox

A comprehensive overview of recognised detox approaches — from dietary cleanses and herbal protocols to medical programmes and lifestyle resets. Tap any method for a step-by-step how-to.

For education, not prescription. Many detox methods lack strong clinical evidence. Medical detox programmes (alcohol, drug) and confirmed parasite or heavy metal protocols should always involve a qualified healthcare provider. Use this as an orientation guide, not a treatment plan.

Dietary & Nutritional

food-based approaches

These detoxes work by modifying what you eat and drink — removing certain foods, replacing meals with liquids, or returning to whole unprocessed ingredients to give the digestive system a rest.

Liquid reset

Juice Detox / Cleanse

Consuming only freshly pressed fruit and vegetable juices for 1–7 days. Removes fibre from the diet, which reduces digestive load while flooding the body with vitamins and antioxidants.

These target specific organ systems or pathogen removal — ranging from colon irrigation to heavy metal chelation to sauna-based sweat protocols. Many require professional guidance.

Medical & Lifestyle

clinical & behavioural detox

These address chemical dependence or behavioural excess — some require medical supervision, others are purely habit-based resets with significant wellbeing benefits.

Substance — medical

Alcohol Detox

Medically supervised withdrawal from alcohol dependence. Severe cases carry life-threatening risks (seizures, delirium tremens) and require clinical management, tapering protocols, and nutritional support — never attempt alone if dependent.

Choosing a detox. The most effective detox for most people is simply reducing ultra-processed foods, alcohol, and added sugars while increasing water, vegetables, fibre, and sleep — allowing the liver, kidneys, and gut to do what they are designed to do. More intensive protocols are best used for specific, identified needs rather than as a general starting point.

Medical detoxes require professional supervision. Alcohol detox, drug detox, and confirmed heavy metal or parasite infections should never be managed without a qualified healthcare provider. See the Parasite Detox guide for the full herb-forward protocol.
